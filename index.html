Filippo Diara

   

Ph.D Geomatics (Topography and Cartography) and Archaeologist (M.Sc, Pg.D.).

Research Technologist and Adjunt Professor at University of Turin. Expert Member of the Comité International de la Photogrammétrie Architecturale (CIPA).

My research activity is about 3D documentation of archaeological and architectural heritage assets: specialized in 3D survey, 3D scanning; 3D modelling, HBIM solutions, CDEs, stratigraphic analysis and building archaeology. Speaker at +35 international conferences, author of +30 scientific papers both in national and international reviews.

           

Projects

Recent projects on which I have worked and collaborated.

Member of S.A.P.TO Selinous Archaeological Project. Selinunte (TP - ITALY). University of Turin - Historical Studies Department. Reference Prof. Marco Serino

Collaboration with the Turkmenistan National Museum (Ashgabat, Turkmenistan) for 3D documentation of Parthian archaeological artefacts.

Collaboration with the Iraq Museum (Baghdad - Iraq) for educational trainings on 3D documentation, scanning and analysis of archaeological artefacts.

Collaboration with the Research and Excavation Center of Turin (CRAST) for 3D documentation of archaeological artefacts from Iraq and Turkmenistan

Collaboration with Asian Art Museum of Turin (MAO) for 3D scanning and analysis of votive terracottas coming from the archaeological site of Seleucia on the Tigris (Iraq).

Collaboration with Boğazköy Muzesi (Çorum - Turkey) for 3D scanning and analysis of cretulae and sealings referred to the Hittite period coming from Ḫattuša.

Collaboration with Musei Reali of Turin for 3D scanning and post-processing analysis of cuneiform tablets and Ancient Near East archaeological artifacts.

WP9 "Taurus" member of ITSERR Resilience project. 3D documentation and analysis of archaeological evidences related to cuneiform clay tablets, seals, sealings of Western Asia.

Architectural requalification of the Frinco Castle (AT). Politecnico di Torino (DAD). Graphic design, 3D modelling and rendering, Stratigraphic analysis.

Collaboration with La Venaria Reale for the restoration project of the historic garden of the Royal Hunting Palace of Stupinigi. Graphic design, 3D modelling and rendering.

Collaboration with Parco Archeologico del Colosseo for the HBIM design and CDE publication of the Giacomo Boni Museum (Roman Forum, Italy).

Development of a modular open source HBIM web platform for Archaeology, through ad-hoc plugins. Designed by using open source instruments.

Collaboration with Sapienza University of Rome for the HBIM design of the Domus Regia (Roman Forum, Italy). Data exchange and publication on cloud solutions.

Management assistant and WP4 member for ERAMCA project. Digital library for the Master's degree in Conservation of Cultural Heritage in Central Asia.

Ph.D project on historical interpretation and HBIM documentation (via open source solutions) of the refectory of Staffarda Abbey (CN, Italy).
