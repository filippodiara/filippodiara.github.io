Filippo Diara

Ph.D in Topography and Cartography and Archaeologist

Ph.D in Topography and Cartography (ICAR/06) - Politecnico di Torino; Researcher; Teaching Assistant for Architecture Courses; G4CH (Geomatics For Cultural Heritage) Laboratory member - DAD department POLITO. His research activity is about documentation of Cultural Heritage assets, especially 3D modeling, HBIM solutions applied to archaeology and historic architecture. He is author of +20 scientific papers both in national and international reviews as well as short novels.

Projects

Recent projects on which I have worked and collaborated

 

La Venaria Reale

Collaboration with La Venaria Reale and Residenze Reali Sabaude (arch. Alessia Bellone and arch. Maurizio Reggi) for the requalification and restoration project of the historic garden of the Royal Hunting Palace of Stupinigi (Torino, TO). Drafting of graphic design (project’ boards) and 3D modelling and rendering of the proposed solution of the garden.

Parco Archeologico del Colosseo

Collaboration with Parco Archeologico del Colosseo (Michela Sediari archaeologist) for the analysis and digital restitution of the Giacomo Boni Museum on the Via Sacra (Roman Forum, Italy). The digital representation is based on the HBIM design of the building and the related publication through cloud solutions.



Sapienza University of Rome

Collaboration with Paolo Carafa and Fabio Cavallero (Sapienza University of Rome) for the HBIM design of the Domus Regia – Sacraria Martis et Opis (Roman Forum, Italy). The religious building was analysed over the years from Sapienza researchers. Project based also on data exchange and publication of the HBIM project on cloud solutions.

ERAMCA - Erasmus+

Collaboration for the ERASMUS+ project ERAMCA (Environmental risk assessment and mitigation on cultural heritage assets in Central Asia). Coordinator: F. Rinaudo. Role: Work Package 4 member and management assistant. Activities: development and dissemination of a digital library on the theoretical content of the Master's degree in Conservation of Cultural Heritage.
